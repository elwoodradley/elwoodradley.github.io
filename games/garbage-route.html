Garbage Route in dev

A thirteen-stop night route. You drive it. At each stop there is something that should not be there, set down with the same care anyone gives the things they love. Your only tool is the log. Write it down and it stays real. Drive past, and it doesn’t — it just stops being witnessed, and gets worse in the dark behind you.

what it is

Witness horror. Not a fight, not a chase — an inventory of the impossible, kept by the one person still bothering to keep it. The dread isn’t a monster in the headlights. It’s that someone cared this much about the wrong thing, too long, with no one watching.

how it plays

Drive the route. Read each stop’s state — what’s finished, what’s been left undone. Choose to log it, leave it, or, where you can, intervene: a small mercy that always costs something, usually carried back to the cab with you. The skill is reading what’s in front of you, not guessing a puzzle.

what it costs you

Ignore enough and the night degrades. The headlight narrows. Perception frays. Stops start to recur. And the log itself — the driver’s own plain voice — slowly takes on the grammar of the things it has been recording. By the back half of the route you may notice you no longer sound like yourself.
